Logo Guide

Logo Methods For Custom Bags

Logo choice affects both appearance and production cost. The right method should match the material, artwork, order quantity, brand positioning and buyer market.

01

Choose Logo Method By Material

Material compatibility should be checked first. Canvas, nylon, Oxford, PVC, EVA, neoprene and PU-like materials do not all support the same branding methods.

  • Flat fabric surfaces are usually easier for printing.
  • Textured materials may work better with woven labels, patches or embroidery.
  • PU-like surfaces can support debossing or metal plate details.
  • Transparent PVC or EVA may require careful logo placement and color planning.
02

Match Logo Style To Market Position

The logo method should support the buyer's market. Promotional giveaways, retail products and premium private-label bags often need different branding strategies.

  • Promotional orders usually need clear, efficient and cost-controlled branding.
  • Retail projects often need a cleaner and more polished logo effect.
  • Premium projects may use metal plates, zipper pull branding or custom labels.
  • Eco or lifestyle projects may prefer woven labels or subtle printed logos.
03

Prepare Artwork Correctly

Logo problems often come from unclear files, wrong colors or missing placement details. Better artwork preparation makes sampling faster.

  • Provide vector artwork when possible.
  • Confirm Pantone color, logo size and placement.
  • Share examples if you prefer a subtle or bold logo effect.
  • Ask for a logo proof before sample production if needed.
04

Review Logo On A Real Sample

A logo may look good on a computer screen but different on the actual fabric. Sample review helps confirm proportion, color and position.

  • Check whether the logo is centered and aligned with seams or pockets.
  • Review logo color under natural light.
  • Confirm durability and whether the method matches the bag usage.
  • Use the approved sample as the bulk production standard.

Branding Strategy

Make The Logo Feel Integrated With The Bag

A strong custom bag does not look like a blank product with a logo added later. The branding method should feel natural for the bag material and buyer market.

  • Material compatibility
  • Artwork clarity
  • Brand positioning
  • Bulk consistency

Buyer Checklist

Before Confirming A Logo Method

Buyers should confirm the practical production details before choosing a logo method only by appearance.

Check Material Fit

The logo process must work with the real fabric or surface.

Check Artwork Detail

Fine lines, gradients and small text may require a different method.

Check Sample Effect

Always review logo placement, color and proportion on the sample.
