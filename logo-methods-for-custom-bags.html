01

Start With Material Compatibility

Not every logo method works well on every material. Canvas, nylon, Oxford, PVC, PU and neoprene all respond differently to branding.

  • Flat fabric panels are easier for printing.
  • Textured fabrics may suit embroidery, labels or patches.
  • PU-like surfaces may support debossing or metal plates.
02

Choose By Brand Positioning

A promotional bag and a premium retail bag should not always use the same branding method.

  • Printing is efficient for simple and cost-focused projects.
  • Embroidery gives a more textured and durable look.
  • Patches and metal plates can create stronger product identity.
03

Check Artwork Complexity

Fine lines, gradients, small text and multi-color artwork affect the best logo method.

  • Simple icons are easier to reproduce consistently.
  • Colorful artwork may need transfer or digital printing.
  • Small detailed marks may work better as labels or plates.
04

Confirm Logo Size And Position

Logo placement should be checked on the actual bag layout, especially when the bag has pockets, seams, handles or curved panels.

  • Front panel logos need enough flat area.
  • Side labels create a more subtle branded look.
  • Zipper pulls, hang tags and packaging can support the main logo.

Branding Selection

Make The Logo Feel Like Part Of The Product

The best branding method should fit the material and strengthen the product style instead of looking like an afterthought.
