Material Guide For Custom Bag Categories

This guide is built around your full custom bag catalog. It helps buyers connect fabric choices with real bag categories, functional needs, logo methods and market positioning.

01

Choose Material By Bag Category

Material selection should begin with the actual bag type. A cosmetic pouch, shopping bag, cooler bag and card binder cannot use the same material logic.

  • Cosmetic and toiletry bags need appearance, lining and touch quality.
  • Shopping and promotional bags need cost-effective strength and logo visibility.
  • Cooler and sports bags need durability, lining and structure.
  • Card binders and OEM cases need protection, stiffness and clean finishing.
02

Compare Function Before Price

A material may look similar in photos but perform very differently in real use. Buyers should compare material function before choosing only by price.

  • Canvas and cotton create a natural reusable look.
  • Nylon, polyester and Oxford are practical for travel, sports and cooler bags.
  • PVC, EVA and TPU are useful for clear, waterproof or easy-clean products.
  • Neoprene, PU and felt can create padded, premium or protective effects.
03

Match Material To Logo Method

Logo performance depends heavily on fabric surface. A logo method that looks good on canvas may not be suitable for PVC or neoprene.

  • Printing is practical for flat fabric panels and promotional orders.
  • Embroidery and woven labels work well for textured or premium styles.
  • Rubber patches, zipper pulls and metal plates can strengthen brand identity.
  • Always test logo appearance on the real production material.
04

Use Samples To Confirm Final Quality

Material names are not enough for custom bag orders. Sample review helps confirm thickness, color, texture, structure, lining and logo effect.

  • Check hand feel, stiffness and whether the bag can stand or fold properly.
  • Review how the material works with zipper, handle, pocket and binding.
  • Confirm color and logo appearance under real lighting.
  • Keep the approved sample as the reference for bulk production.
